About SANGAMA Symposium

A Global Confluence of Healing Wisdom

SANGAMA (confluence) is envisioned as a first-of-its-kind international platform that brings together global leaders across Modern Oncology, Ayurveda, Yoga, Naturopathy, and Integrative Medicine to advance a unified, evidence-based approach to cancer care.

This two-day summit will convene leading oncologists, national experts, and academic institutions, along with 200+ high-potential postgraduate researchers from across India and the globe.

The program is designed as a capacity-building and policy-informing platform, positioning India at the forefront of integrative oncology innovation and research leadership.

About Kalaburagi

A Land of Spiritual & Historical Harmony

The recorded history of Kalaburagi dates back to the 6th century CE. Over centuries, the region came under the rule of several prominent dynasties including the Chalukyas, Rashtrakutas, Kalachuris, Yadavas, Kakatiyas, Bahmanis, Adil Shahis, Mughals, and the Nizams of Hyderabad.

Kalaburagi attained special prominence in the 14th century when it became the first capital of the Bahmani Sultanate, one of the earliest independent Islamic kingdoms in the Deccan. The Bahmani rulers left behind remarkable monuments, forts, mosques, domes, and tombs that continue to attract historians, architects, and tourists from across the country.

The district is also deeply connected with the social reform and spiritual movements of Karnataka. The traditions of the Sharanas, Sufi saints, saints of the Bhakti movement, and Buddhist influences have all enriched the cultural fabric of the region.

Places of interest

Explore Local Heritage

Kalaburagi Fort

Built originally by the Kakatiyas and strengthened by Bahmani rulers. Features massive granite walls, bastions, and the historic Jama Masjid, inspired by the Mosque of Córdoba.

Khwaja Banda Nawaz Dargah

One of the most respected Sufi pilgrimage centres in South India, drawing lakhs of pilgrims for the annual Urs festival, symbolizing peace and communal harmony.

Sharana Basaveshwara Temple

Dedicated to the 18th-century saint-philosopher, this temple is a vital spiritual institution known for its teachings on equality and large-scale charitable activities.

Buddha Vihar

One of the largest Buddhist centres in India, featuring a grand Buddha statue and serene atmosphere for meditation and study of Buddhist philosophy.

Sannati Buddhist Site

A significant archaeological site on the Bhima River, featuring ancient stupas and the only surviving image of Emperor Ashoka discovered anywhere in the world.

Haft Gumbaz

Meaning "Seven Domes," this complex of tombs belongs to Bahmani rulers, showcasing an elegant fusion of Persian and Deccan architectural styles.

Malkhed Fort

Once Manyakheta, the capital of the mighty Rashtrakuta Empire. A historic centre of political power, culture, and learning in Indian history.

"Kalaburagi is a unique land where history, spirituality, agriculture, and cultural harmony blend seamlessly. From the grandeur of Bahmani monuments and the Buddhist legacy of Sannati to the fragrance of tur fields and the message of communal coexistence, the district offers a distinctive identity that reflects the inclusive spirit and heritage of Karnataka and India."

Landmark Initiative

ONCOLAB Foundation Stone Laying Ceremony

During SANGAMA Onco Conclave 2026, Shri JP Nadda, Hon'ble Union Minister for Health & Family Welfare, will lay the foundation stone for ONCOLAB — a comprehensive oncology diagnostics and research laboratory at Arijeeva Healthcare Hospital, Kalaburagi.

ONCOLAB will provide advanced cancer diagnostics including molecular profiling, genomic testing, and immunohistochemistry — services currently unavailable in the region — eliminating the need for patients to travel 600+ kilometers to Bangalore or Hyderabad for essential cancer tests.

The facility will also house a dedicated Oral Cancer Research Laboratory focused on studying disease patterns unique to North Karnataka, developing early detection biomarkers, and evaluating preventive strategies for tobacco-related oral cancers. This landmark initiative represents a transformative step in making world-class oncology diagnostics accessible to underserved populations across North Karnataka while advancing cancer research that benefits patients throughout India.
