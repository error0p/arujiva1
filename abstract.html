Call for Abstracts — SANGAMA Onco Conclave 2026

We invite postgraduate students, early-career researchers, and faculty from Medical, Dental, AYUSH and allied health sciences to submit abstracts for oral and poster presentations at SANGAMA Onco Conclave 2026. Showcase your research on integrative oncology and compete for attractive prizes.

Eligibility

  • ✓ Open to postgraduate students (MD, MS, MDS, BAMS, BNYS, BUMS, BHMS, PhD scholars) and early-career researchers from Medical, Dental, and AYUSH systems
  • ✓ Research in oncology, integrative cancer care, supportive care, palliative medicine, and related fields
  • ✓ Original research only — all abstracts will be screened for plagiarism
  • ✓ Each author may submit maximum TWO abstracts (one oral + one poster, or two posters)
  • ✓ At least one author must register and attend the conclave to present
  • ✓ Postgraduate students MUST upload a letter from Head of Department certifying their PG student status (downloadable template provided below)

Presentation Formats

Format Details
🎤 Oral Presentation 10-minute presentation + 2-minute Q&A. PowerPoint slides allowed (max 10 slides). Top 20 abstracts selected for oral presentation.
🖼️ Poster Presentation A1 size (594 × 841 mm), portrait orientation. Posters displayed throughout the conclave. Authors present during dedicated poster session on Day 1 evening. Top 30–40 abstracts selected for poster.

Abstract Format & Submission Guidelines

  • 1Abstract must be structured: Title, Background, Objective, Methods, Results, Conclusion
  • 2Maximum word count: 300 words (excluding title and author names)
  • 3Font: Arial or Times New Roman, 12-point
  • 4File format: Submit as Word (.docx) or PDF
  • 5Include 3–5 keywords at the end of the abstract
  • 6Author details: Name, affiliation, contact email, mobile number
  • 7Clearly state if the abstract has been presented or published elsewhere
  • 8Mention if the study has received ethics committee approval (for human/animal studies)

Selection Process

  • 1All abstracts reviewed by a scientific committee of senior faculty from multiple disciplines
  • 2Judging criteria: Originality (30%), Methodology (30%), Relevance to integrative oncology (20%), Clarity of presentation (20%)
  • 3Top 20 abstracts selected for ORAL presentation
  • 4Next 30–40 abstracts selected for POSTER presentation
  • 5Acceptance notification sent via email 2 weeks before the conclave
  • 6Authors must register and pay the registration fee even if their abstract is accepted
Originality
30%

Novel research question, unique approach, new contribution to the field.

Methodology
30%

Rigorous study design, appropriate methods, valid statistical analysis.

Relevance
20%

Alignment with integrative oncology themes and clinical significance.

Presentation Quality
20%

Clarity of writing, structured format, logical flow and conciseness.
